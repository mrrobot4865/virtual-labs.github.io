Theory: Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) states that the sum of all voltages around any closed loop in a circuit is equal to zero. Mathematically, this can be expressed as: ∑V=0 This means that the total amount of voltage gained in a loop (from power sources) is equal to the total voltage dropped across the resistors or other components.
